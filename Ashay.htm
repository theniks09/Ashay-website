Can Kidney Stones Be Treated Without Surgery?

Many small kidney stones can pass naturally with the right medicines and adequate hydration.

Medical Expulsion Therapy (MET), pain control and follow-up scans are used to help stones move safely without surgery when it is appropriate to do so.

Medical Management of Enlarged Prostate (BPH)

Do I need surgery for my enlarged prostate? Not necessarily.

Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) is a common condition in aging men. Many patients fear that a diagnosis means immediate surgery. However, modern urology focuses heavily on medical management first.

How We Treat Without Surgery:

For mild to moderate symptoms, we start with medications such as alpha-blockers and 5-alpha reductase inhibitors, which relax muscles and gradually shrink the prostate.

Benefits of Medication:

  • Improved urinary flow and reduced frequency.
  • Avoids hospitalization and anesthesia.
  • Minimal side effects compared to surgery.

Surgery (TURP) is only recommended if: medication fails, there is recurrent retention, recurrent infections, or kidney damage. Surgery is a carefully weighed decision.

When is Surgery Actually Necessary?

"The best surgery is the one you don't need."

The role of a surgeon is also to know when not to operate. Many urological problems have a spectrum of conservative and surgical options.

Our Honest Approach:

  • Diagnosis First: Confirm the problem with appropriate scans and tests.
  • Conservative Trial: Try medicines and lifestyle changes where safe.
  • Shared Decision Making: Explain pros and cons so patients decide with full information.

Some situations still need timely surgery to prevent kidney or bladder damage. The goal is to balance long-term safety with minimally invasive care.

What is Premature Ejaculation?

Premature ejaculation is a common male sexual problem where a man climaxes earlier than he or his partner would like, often within a very short time after penetration or even before intercourse starts.

Is Premature Ejaculation Harmful?

It is usually not dangerous to physical health but can cause stress, low confidence, relationship issues and performance anxiety if not addressed properly.

Common Causes

  • Performance anxiety and overthinking during intercourse.
  • Irregular sexual activity or masturbation patterns.
  • Certain medical or hormonal conditions in some patients.
  • Relationship stress or psychological factors.

Safe Treatment Options

  • Counselling and education in a private and non-judgmental environment.
  • Behavioural techniques that help improve control and confidence.
  • Use of selected medicines or topical agents when suitable.
  • Evaluation for associated problems like erectile dysfunction or low testosterone if needed.

Why Consult a Urologist / Andrologist?

Self-medication, random online tablets and local creams can be unsafe. A trained urologist and andrologist can identify the real cause and offer a safe, individualised treatment plan.

Understanding Erectile Dysfunction (ED)

Erectile Dysfunction is the inability to get or keep an erection firm enough for sexual intercourse. It can be a sign of a physical or psychological condition. It is not something to be embarrassed about, as it is highly treatable.

Common Causes

  • Physical: Heart disease, diabetes, obesity, high blood pressure, low testosterone.
  • Psychological: Stress, anxiety, depression, relationship issues.
  • Lifestyle: Smoking, alcohol use, lack of exercise.

Treatment Options

We provide a comprehensive evaluation to find the root cause. Treatments may include lifestyle changes, oral medications, hormone therapy, or advanced options like shockwave therapy or penile implants depending on severity.

Kidney Stone Treatments

We specialize in advanced laser surgeries for kidney stones, ensuring minimal pain and faster recovery. However, medical management is always the first line of treatment for smaller stones.

Procedures Offered:

  • RIRS (Retrograde Intrarenal Surgery): Flexible scope laser surgery for kidney stones without any cut in selected cases.
  • URSL (Ureteroscopic Lithotripsy): For stones in the ureter.
  • PCNL (Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy): Keyhole surgery for large and difficult kidney stones.

Urethral Stricture Treatment

Narrowing of the urine pipe (stricture) causes difficulty in passing urine. We offer both endoscopic and reconstructive solutions.

Treatments:
